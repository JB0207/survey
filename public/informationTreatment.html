The world map illustrates whether the climate actions initiated or planned by governments are meeting the globally agreed aim of the Paris Climate Agreement to keep global warming well below 2°C and to continue efforts to limit warming to 1.5°C.
As can be seen on the map, none of the countries shown, which emit 85% of the world's greenhouse gas emissions, have taken sufficient action to meet the climate targets of the Paris Climate Agreement.



Climate Analytics and the New Climate Institute, the creators of the world map, predict with currently adopted and planned climate change mitigation measures a warming of 2.7°C by 2100 and the renowned Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) a global warming of 3°C by 2100.
